Hapur’s Housing Story—From Connectivity Upgrade to Better Livability

Hapur’s residential market sits at the intersection of:
• Connectivity
• Affordability
• Ownership
Over the last few years, multiple expressway and highway upgrades (Delhi–Meerut Expressway Ph 3 Dasna–Hapur; NH 9 six laning Hapur–Moradabad) have compressed commute times and expanded the effective residential catchment of Delhi NCR into Hapur. This, coupled with availability of land parcels under freehold rights and abundant housing stock are key attractions for investment. Furthermore, HPDA’s GIS based Master Plan 2031, has unlocked both greenfield and infill opportunities under predictable land use controls.

Growth Indicators:

There has been a steep rise in authority revenue—from ₹172.07 Cr (FY 2023 24) to ₹443.39 Cr (FY 2024 25)—as plan approvals, plot allotments, and scheme monetization have accelerated. That revenue traction signals both market depth and administrative throughput for housing.
The Master Plan 2031 allocates ~51.27% of land to residential use, ~9.60% to industrial, and ~10.41% to transportation—creating a balanced urban fabric that supports neighborhood viability and price stability. The plan phases development across Hapur & Pilkhuwa first, then Brijghat & Garh Mukteshwar, accommodating growth toward the Ganga Expressway corridor.

Existing Schemes & Stock:

Multiple housing schemes, namely Anand Vihar, Preet Vihar 1 and 2, PMAY, Haripur Yojana (proposed), with good quality services including roads, drains, electrification, STPs, and amenities, indicate the building housing stock and improving liveability indices at growing pace. Upcoming group housing projects promise executable inventory for end users and investors.

Demand Drivers

• Land ownership and connectivity: Hapur offers land ownership at better rates against Noida and Greater Noida, with <1 hour access to Delhi via expressway.
• Industrial job nodes: HPDA’s existing and proposed industrial areas (Dheer Khera, Pilkhuwa Textile Centre, NH 24 Bypass) create localized housing demand.
• Tourism & riverfront: Garh Mukteshwar’s riverfront and temple circuit can catalyze mixed residential—second homes, senior living, rental apartments.
