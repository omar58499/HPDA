Development and Achievements of Hapur in recent years

Infrastructure and Connectivity Overhaul

A key focus has been the massive improvement in regional connectivity:

  • National Highway 9 (NH-9) Expansion: The critical Hapur ‚Äì Moradabad section of the highway (formerly NH-24) was upgraded to a 6-lane access-controlled highway. This significantly reduced travel time and improved logistical efficiency between Delhi-NCR and Eastern UP/Uttarakhand.
  • Delhi Meerut Expressway: The expressway includes a dedicated 22 km stretch from Dasna to Hapur (Phase 3), making Hapur directly connected to Delhi and NCR through a high-speed, signal-free route. This has drastically reduced travel time between Hapur and Delhi from 2‚Äì3 hours to under 1 hour. The construction of the overbridge at Pilkuwa has eliminated frequent traffic jam.
  • Targeted Investment: In late 2022, the state government inaugurated 274 development projects valued at approximately ‚Çπ810 crore, primarily targeting the improvement of roads, drainage, and basic utilities across the district.
  • Ganga Expressway Impact: Hapur is strategically placed to benefit from the new Ganga Expressway (Meerut to Prayagraj), which is set to unlock significant land for industrial and logistics growth in areas like the Garhmukteshwar Tehsil.

Economic and Industrial Growth

Hapur's role as a major manufacturing hub has been reinforced:

  • Industrial Corridor Development: The Uttar Pradesh Expressways Industrial Development Authority (UPEIDA) is actively developing an Integrated Logistics and Manufacturing Cluster near Bahadurpur (Garhmukteshwar) along the upcoming Ganga Expressway, involving substantial land acquisition for new industries.
  • Sectoral Performance: The district recorded a strong overall economic growth rate, with the secondary (industrial) sector increasing its share, reflecting growth in manufacturing. The primary (agriculture) sector also showed robust annual growth of 30.47%.
  • Support for Local Industries: Hapur continues to be known for Stainless Steel Pipes & Tubes and the handloom/powerloom industry of Pilkhuwa (home furnishings). These industries have received focused state support under the One District One Product (ODOP) scheme.

Urban Planning and Civic Infrastructure

The Hapur-Pilkhuwa Development Authority (HPDA) has been instrumental in planned urban expansion:

  • Master Plan 2031: The HPDA is actively implementing the Hapur Master Plan 2031, which provides the framework for regulated urban expansion, land-use control, and infrastructure development to manage population growth. HPDA has significantly contributed to the region's infrastructure transformation through projects focusing on road construction, STPs, drainage, community centres, street lighting, market spaces, and basic service hubs.
  • Planned Housing: New housing and commercial projects like Anand Vihar and Preet Vihar have been developed to accommodate the increasing urban population. Recent works include tenders for a Convention Centre and ongoing maintenance of these urban schemes.
  • Upgraded Municipal Services: Funds from the 15th Finance Commission have been directed towards critical municipal works, including the repair of roads and sewers, and investment in modern equipment for Solid Waste Management and potentially new Water Treatment Plants (WTP).
